\beginsection Distributions of functions of a random variable

Example:
$$f(x)=2xe^{-x^2}$$
$$Y=X^2$$
Solution:
$$G(y)=P(Y\le y)=P(X^2\le y)=P(X\le\sqrt y)$$
Hence
$$G(y)=\int_0^{\sqrt y}2xe^{-x^2}\,dx=-e^{-x^2}\bigg|_0^{\sqrt y}=-e^{-y}+1$$
$$f(y)={dG\over dy}=e^{-y}$$

\beginsection Special case when $f(x)\sim U(0,1)$

In this case $f(x)=1/(b-a)=1$ so no integral is required. We have
$$G(y)=\sqrt y$$
Proof:
$$G(y)=\int_0^{\sqrt y}1\,dx=x\bigg|_0^{\sqrt y}=\sqrt y$$

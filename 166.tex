{\bf F Distribution}

\bigskip
It is the ratio of two chi-square distributions.
$${\chi_j^2/j\over\chi_k^2/k}={k\over j}\times{\chi_j^2\over\chi_k^2}\sim F_{j,k}$$
$${1\over F_{j,k}}\sim F_{k,j}$$
$$\mu={k\over k-2}$$
$$\sigma^2={2k^2(j+k-2)\over j(k-2)^2(k-4)}$$

\bigskip
Example 1
$${(X_1-\mu)^2\over\sigma^2}\sim\chi_1^2,\qquad
{(X_{64}-\mu)^2\over\sigma^2}\sim\chi_1^2,\qquad
{(X_1-\mu)^2\over(X_{64}-\mu)^2}\sim F_{1,1}$$

\bigskip
Example 2
$$
{\sum_{i=1}^{64}(X_i-\mu_X)^2\over\sigma_X^2}\sim\chi_{64}^2,\quad
{\sum_{j=1}^{100}(Y_j-\mu_Y)^2\over\sigma_Y^2}\sim\chi_{100}^2,\quad
{
100\sigma_Y^2\sum_{i=1}^{64}(X_i-\mu_X)^2
\over
64\sigma_X^2\sum_{j=1}^{100}(Y_j-\mu_Y)^2
}\sim F_{64,100}
$$

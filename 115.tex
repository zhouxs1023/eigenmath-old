\beginsection 3.

Consider the continuous random variable $X$ with
distribution function as
$$F(t)=\cases{
0, & $t<0$\cr
0.75t+0.25t^3, & $0\le t<1$\cr
1, & $1\le t$
}$$

\bigskip
3a) Find the probability density function of $X$.
$$f(x)={\partial F(x)\over\partial x}=0.75+0.75x^2,\qquad0\le x<1$$

\bigskip
3b) Find the moment generating function of $X$.
$$\eqalign{
E[e^{tX}]&=\int_0^1e^{tx}(0.75+0.75x^2)\,dx\cr
&=0.75\int_0^1e^{tx}\,dx+0.75\int_0^1x^2e^{tx}\,dx\cr
&=0.75\left({e^{tx}\over t}+{e^{tx}(t^2x^2-2tx+2)\over t^3}\right)\bigg|_0^1\cr
&={1.5e^t-1.5\over t^3}-{1.5e^t\over t^2}+{1.5e^t-0.75\over t}
}$$
Since $E[e^{tX}]$ is not defined for $t=0$, the moment
generating function $M(t)$ does not exist.

\bigskip
3c) Find the variance of $X$.
$$E[X]=\int_0^1x(0.75+0.75x^2)\,dx=0.75\left({1\over2}x^2+{1\over4}x^4\right)\bigg|_0^1
=0.75^2=0.5625$$
$$E[X^2]=\int_0^1x^2(0.75+0.75x^2)\,dx=0.75\left({1\over3}x^3+{1\over5}x^5\right)\bigg|_0^1
=0.4$$
$$\sigma^2=E[X^2]-(E[X])^2=0.0836$$

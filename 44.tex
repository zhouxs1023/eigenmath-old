1.1 Express 33 dBrnC of noise in terms of picowatts and milliwatts of
absolute power.

\bigskip

This is the relation of dBrnC to C-weighted picowatts.
$$33\,{\rm dBrnC}=10\log_{10}\left({P\over1\,{\rm pWC}}\right)$$
Solving for $P$ we get
$$P=10^{3.3}=2000\,{\rm pWC}$$
Divide by one billion to convert picowatts to milliwatts.
$$(2000\,{\rm pWC})\times(10^{-9}\,{\rm mWC/pWC})=2\times10^{-6}\,{\rm mWC}$$

\bigskip

Note: Here is one nanowatt measured in both dBrn and dBm.
$$10\log_{10}\left({10^{-9}\over10^{-12}}\right)=30\,{\rm dBrn}
\qquad10\log_{10}\left({10^{-9}\over10^{-6}}\right)=-60\,{\rm dBm}$$
